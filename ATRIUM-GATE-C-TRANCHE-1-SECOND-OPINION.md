## Summary

I concur with **all eight proposed dispositions** (2 retain, 5 revise,
1 remove). The internal briefs are calibrated, correctly distinguish
ancient testimony from modern reconstruction, and err on the side the
evidence errs. I add four substantive strengthenings, one of which
(case 4) affects what tranche 2 should contain.

## Per-case verdicts

### 1. `edge-eleatic-milesian` — CONCUR with *revise*
The denial of generation and perishing in Parmenides B8 is read by an
influential interpretive tradition as targeting Ionian cosmogony, but
no documented school controversy exists — "Eleatic school" is itself
partly retrospective (Plato, *Sophist* 242d). The proposed note's
"historiographic relation, not evidence of a documented controversy"
is exactly right. *Optional strengthening:* the rationale's
acknowledgment of constructive readings of Parmenidean cosmology
(Mourelatos, Curd line) deserves to reach the published note text, not
just the rationale.

### 2. `edge-xenophanes-eleatic` — CONCUR with *revise*, no reservations
The genealogy rests on Aristotle (*Met.* A5 986b — with his own hedge
that Xenophanes "made nothing clear") and the doxographic tradition;
Plato's *Sophist* 242d treats the lineage half-jokingly. The proposed
note preserves the ancient reception while denying the teacher–student
and continuous-school claims the fragments cannot support. Precisely
calibrated.

### 3. `edge-heraclitus-stoa` — CONCUR with *retain*, one hedge required
The reception is among the best-attested facts in the file (Cleanthes'
four books of *Interpretations of Heraclitus*, DL 7.174; designing
fire; logos). **However, the proposed note attributes "cosmic
recurrence" to the appropriated Heraclitean material.** Whether
Heraclitus himself held ekpyrosis is an old controversy (the Stoicizing
doxography asserts it; a strong modern line, Kirk onward, denies it).
Recommend the note read "…including fire and, on the Stoic reading,
cosmic recurrence…" — otherwise the corpus quietly takes sides in
exactly the way this packet exists to prevent.

### 4. `edge-pyrrhonism-academic` — CONCUR with *remove*, with a
**directional finding for tranche 2**
The removal reasoning is correct: the node denotes the movement
(Aenesidemus's revival onward), which **post-dates** Arcesilaus; the
genuinely disputed question concerns the person Pyrrho (Ariston's
"Plato the head, Pyrrho the tail" jibe, DL 4.33; SEP treats influence
on Arcesilaus as possible and disputed). But note what the graph loses:
the *well-attested* relation between these two nodes runs in the
**opposite direction** — Aenesidemus was an Academic who broke away to
found the Pyrrhonist revival (Photius, *Bibliotheca* 212). Tranche 2
should consider: (a) an `Academic Skepticism → Pyrrhonism` edge
(origin/reaction, high confidence), and (b) the distinct Pyrrho person
node the brief already anticipates, carrying the contested edge to
Arcesilaus. Pure removal is right for 0.3.x; the reversed edge is the
truth the map wants to teach.

### 5. `edge-stoa-plotinus` — CONCUR with *revise*; **add the primary anchor**
"Appropriation within sustained disagreement" is the correct formula
(*Enn.* IV.7 against corporeal soul; VI.1–3 against Stoic categories).
But the case misses its strongest evidence: **Porphyry, *Life of
Plotinus* 14 — explicit ancient testimony that Stoic and Peripatetic
doctrines are blended, "concealed," in Plotinus's writings.** That is
direct testimony, not reconstruction; it belongs in the evidence
anchors and arguably bears on the E-level. Direction of the revision
(synthesis → influence) unaffected — VP 14 attests presence of Stoic
material, not a systematic synthesis.

### 6. `edge-iamblichean-porphyry-critique` — CONCUR with *retain*,
no reservations
The *Reply to Porphyry* (De Mysteriis, answering the *Letter to
Anebo*) is direct, surviving, and targeted. High confidence, E1,
dependence-with-critique note: all correct.

### 7. `edge-iamblichean-alexandria` — CONCUR with *revise*; phrasing note
Mediation and the high→medium downgrade are right. The flat listing
"including Syrianus, Proclus, Hermeias, and Ammonius" avoids asserting
a strict chain, which is acceptable — but the actual structure is
cleaner and worth stating: Hermeias studied under Syrianus (alongside
Proclus, not under him); Ammonius, Hermeias's son, studied under
Proclus. Suggested phrasing: "through the Athenian school (Syrianus,
Proclus) and its Alexandrian heirs (Hermeias, Ammonius)." The
adaptation claim (distinct religious-pedagogical setting) is well
supported by the Ammonius material.

### 8. `edge-porphyry-augustine` — CONCUR with *revise*, no reservations
The note's separation is exactly the scholarly state of play: secure
later engagement (*City of God* X, *De regressu animae* quotations)
versus the disputed contents of the Milanese "libri Platonicorum"
(*Conf.* 7.9.13; the Courcelle/O'Meara-versus-Plotinus-primacy
debate). Keeping `contested` is correct.
