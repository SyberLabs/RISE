# Scripture imagery classification — all 73 books

The Atrium's four-class pass applied to the Chapel's canon
(2026-07-22). The question per book: what imagery, if any, honestly
belongs behind this reading? "No assignment" must never silently mean
"whatever exists" — a book without a class reads in stillness, which
is a liturgical register, not a gap.

Classes:

- **SERVED** — carried by an existing chapel collection (book- or
  chapter-level assignment already live in handoff.js)
- **DEPICTED** — the book's narratives are richly painted subjects;
  a pinned collection is worth building
- **CONCEPTUAL** — the book is law, wisdom, or epistle: not a depicted
  subject. Stillness now; the Christian procedural forms (spec §6,
  stage 7) are the natural companions
- **CYCLE** — coverable by a single coherent engraved cycle (Doré)
  as one behind-stream voice, where individual masterpieces are thin

A book may carry a primary class and a cycle note. Assignments are
made from the text's own content, chapter ranges noted where a future
chapter-level map would differ from the book default.

## Old Testament

| Book | Class | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Genesis | DEPICTED | The richest painted book in the canon: Creation, Eden, the Flood, Babel, Abraham and Isaac, Jacob's Ladder, Joseph cycle. → `chapel-patriarchs` |
| Exodus | DEPICTED | Moses: burning bush, plagues, Red Sea, Sinai, the golden calf. → `chapel-patriarchs` (Moses anchors it) |
| Leviticus | CONCEPTUAL | Law code; no narrative surface. Stillness. |
| Numbers | CONCEPTUAL | Census and law with narrative islands (brazen serpent, Balaam) too thin for a collection. Stillness; CYCLE note. |
| Deuteronomy | CONCEPTUAL | Moses' discourse. Stillness; the death of Moses is a CYCLE plate. |
| Josue | CYCLE | Jericho and the campaigns: painted rarely, engraved often. |
| Judges | DEPICTED (thin) | Samson carries it (Rubens, Rembrandt, van Dyck); Deborah and Gideon are cycle material. Candidate for `chapel-prophets` widening or its own small set later. |
| Ruth | DEPICTED (thin) | Ruth and Boaz gleaning — a beloved subject (Poussin, Millet is post-cutoff). Small; cycle covers. |
| 1–4 Kings | DEPICTED | Saul and David (1–2 Kings): David and Goliath, Saul and David's harp, Bathsheba, Absalom. Elijah and Elisha (3–4 Kings): ravens, Carmel, the chariot. → `chapel-prophets` for Elijah/Elisha; David material strong. |
| 1–2 Paralipomenon | CONCEPTUAL | Chronicle recapitulation; the paintable scenes belong to Kings. Stillness. |
| 1 Esdras / Nehemias | CYCLE | Return and rebuilding: engraved, rarely painted. |
| Tobias | DEPICTED | Tobias and the Angel — a whole Netherlandish/Italian genre (Rembrandt's workshop returned to it constantly). → `chapel-prophets` widening or later set. |
| Judith | DEPICTED | Judith and Holofernes: Gentileschi (post-cutoff versions vary), Cranach, Mantegna. Powerful but violent — curate with the room's register in mind. |
| Esther | DEPICTED | Esther before Assuerus (Rembrandt, Poussin, Gentileschi). |
| 1–2 Machabees | CYCLE | Battle narrative; engraved cycles cover it. |
| Job | DEPICTED | Job on the dunghill, Job and his friends: Bonnat is late, but La Tour, Fouquet miniatures, Blake (1826, in range). → `chapel-prophets` candidate. |
| Psalms | SERVED (stillness) + note | King David harping exists abundantly, but the Psalter is prayed, not narrated. DELIBERATE stillness; the Marian/Pantocrator icon focal is its natural companion. Revisit after chant. |
| Proverbs / Ecclesiastes / Canticles / Wisdom / Ecclesiasticus | CONCEPTUAL | Wisdom literature; not depicted subjects (Canticles' bridal imagery is allegorized in paint mostly as Marian iconography, already served by icons). Stillness → procedural forms at stage 7. |
| Isaias | DEPICTED | The prophet himself (Michelangelo's Sistine Isaiah is fresco-in-situ — engravings after it work), the vision of the seraph, the peaceable kingdom. → `chapel-prophets` |
| Jeremias | DEPICTED | Rembrandt's Jeremiah Lamenting (Rijksmuseum SK-A-3276!) is the single strongest OT painting available to us. → `chapel-prophets` anchor |
| Lamentations | DEPICTED (via Jeremias) | The same Rembrandt serves; no separate set. |
| Baruch | CONCEPTUAL | Stillness. |
| Ezechiel | DEPICTED (thin) | The vision of the wheels and the dry bones: mostly engraved (Doré's dry bones is canonical). CYCLE primary. |
| Daniel | DEPICTED | Belshazzar's Feast (Rembrandt), Daniel in the lions' den (Rubens), Susanna, the fiery furnace. → `chapel-prophets` |
| Osee–Malachias (the Twelve) | CYCLE | Jonas excepted: the whale, Nineveh, the gourd are painted AND engraved — Jonas joins `chapel-prophets`. The rest read in stillness or cycle. |

## New Testament

| Book | Class | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Matthew / Mark / Luke / John | SERVED | Passion + Crucifixion book-level; Nativity chapters 1–3 (Mt, Lk), Mk 1; Resurrection chapters (Mt 28, Mk 16, Lk 24, Jn 20–21). Chapter map may deepen later (e.g. parables, miracles as a future set). |
| Acts | DEPICTED | Pentecost, the stoning of Stephen, the Conversion of Saul (Caravaggio's is in situ; engravings and other masters serve), Peter freed from prison (Raphael's is fresco; Honthorst's Peter works). Worth a set when the NT pass comes. |
| Romans–Philemon (Pauline) | CONCEPTUAL | Epistles: argument, not scene. Stillness → procedural forms. A single pinned Rembrandt "Paul at his desk" as an icon-like focal is a tasteful future option. |
| Hebrews | CONCEPTUAL | Stillness. |
| James–Jude (Catholic epistles) | CONCEPTUAL | Stillness. |
| Apocalypse | SERVED + CYCLE note | `chapel-resurrection` book-level today. Dürer's Apocalypse woodcut cycle (1498, PD-certain) is the canonical CYCLE upgrade — fifteen sheets, one voice, made FOR this text. Strong stage-7-adjacent candidate. |

## Sequencing

1. **Now:** `chapel-prophets` (Elijah, Elisha, Isaias, Jeremias,
   Daniel, Jonas, Job anchors) + `chapel-patriarchs` (Genesis/Exodus:
   Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Joseph, Moses). Harvested from Met,
   Rijksmuseum, Cleveland, AIC — the adapters that exist.
2. **Doré evaluation:** one contact sheet of representative plates
   across the canon. If the cycle earns its place on sight, it becomes
   the CYCLE class's single implementation — full-canon coverage in
   one voice, assigned only to books classified CYCLE (never
   displacing a painted collection).
3. **Later, deliberately:** Judith/Esther/Tobias/Samson sets; Acts;
   Dürer's Apocalypse; the Pauline focal.

## Standing rules (unchanged)

Rights gates in full; contact-sheet review before any pin; chapel-*
scoping with stillness-not-fallback; procedural forms accompany and
never depict the face of Christ; absence is a register, not an error.
